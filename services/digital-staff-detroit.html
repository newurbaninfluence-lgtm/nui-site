The Problem

Every Hour You're Unavailable,
Someone Else Gets the Job.

Your business only exists when you're working. When you're on a job, in a meeting, asleep, or with your family — the phone rings and nobody answers. The lead fills out a form and waits. The customer who needed a quick answer goes to the next result on Google.

Missed calls are missed revenue

A customer calls, hits voicemail, and calls the next business on the list. They're not loyal to you yet. They go to whoever picks up. That's not laziness on their part — that's just how buying decisions work.

Slow follow-up loses the job

Studies show that responding to a lead within 5 minutes vs. 30 minutes increases your contact rate by more than 100x. Most businesses follow up in hours — or days. By then the person moved on.

You're doing work that shouldn't need you

Answering the same questions. Sending confirmations. Booking appointments. Following up on estimates. None of this requires your expertise — it just consumes your time and attention.

Voicemail is not a business system

A recording that says "leave a message" is not a solution. Customers in 2025 expect a response in minutes, not a callback promise. Voicemail is where sales go to die.

Questions

What People
Actually Ask.

Will my customers know they're talking to AI?

That depends on how you want to set it up. Some businesses are transparent about it — "Hi, you've reached [Business Name], I'm [Name], the team's AI assistant." Others program it to handle the conversation as a staff member without the distinction. Either way, the experience has to be good enough that it doesn't matter. A well-programmed Digital Staff is indistinguishable from a great employee.

Can it actually handle a full phone call conversation?

Yes. Not a menu system — an actual conversation. It asks and answers questions, handles objections, books appointments, and manages the entire intake flow. If something comes up outside its knowledge base or decision authority, it escalates to you with a full summary so you're walking in with context.

How do we program the brand voice?

We start with a deep-dive on your brand: how you talk to customers, how you handle specific scenarios, your service descriptions, your most common questions, your tone. We write the scripts. We program the decision logic. We run test conversations until it sounds right. Then it goes live — and keeps improving from there.

What if I want it to sound like me specifically?

That's exactly the goal. We'll use real examples of how you communicate — emails you've written, calls you've had, your social media voice — to calibrate it to your specific patterns. It'll reason the way you reason, handle situations the way you handle them, and escalate the things you'd want to weigh in on yourself.

Do I need a Digital HQ first?

Yes. Digital Staff runs on the infrastructure your HQ provides — the website, CRM, lead capture, and booking system are what the staff operates from. HQ Lite unlocks the Digital Secretary. HQ Standard unlocks the Lead Catcher and Street Announcer.

What's the difference between this and a chatbot?

Night and day. A chatbot answers from a fixed menu. Digital Staff reasons through open-ended conversations, handles situations it hasn't seen before, learns from outcomes, and operates across phone, email, SMS, and web — not just a chat widget. It's not a tool. It's a staff member.
