The Problem

Customers Where They Are.

Social media platforms block your ads. Google Maps shows your competitor first. Your content is inconsistent. Your audience list is empty. And the people who should be finding you are finding someone else.

Platform ad restrictions

Facebook and Google have content restrictions that hit nightlife, cannabis, and other industries hard. You try to run an ad, it gets flagged. You try to boost a post, it gets half the reach. Most businesses give up and lose to competitors who found a way around it.

Your social media looks dead

Customers check your Instagram before they call. If the last post was 3 months ago, they move on. An inconsistent, low-quality social presence signals that your business isn't active — or worse, isn't serious.

You're invisible on Google Maps

You show up when someone searches for your exact business name. You disappear when they search for what you do in neighborhoods two miles away. Territory coverage is a game you're not playing.

Your audience is rented, not owned

Every follower on Instagram can disappear overnight if the algorithm changes or your account gets flagged. A phone number and push subscription belong to you. Nobody can take that list away.

Questions

Common Questions.

Can bars and clubs use geofencing even with ad restrictions?

Yes — and this is one of the most important things to understand. Geofencing uses programmatic display advertising, not Facebook Ads or Google Ads. Platform content restrictions do not apply. For nightlife, cannabis, adult venues, and any other restricted industry, geofencing is one of the only paid visibility tools that works without fighting the algorithm.

What's the difference between the Digital Promoter and the Content Crew?

The Content Crew handles your social media — the content people see when they browse your profile or feed. The Digital Promoter handles your owned audience — SMS, push notifications, and retargeting for people who already opted in to hear from you. The Crew builds your public presence. The Promoter activates the people who already said yes.

What's geo-gridding and how is it different from geofencing?

Geofencing targets people who physically enter a specific location. Geo-gridding targets the Google Maps ranking game — systematically building your presence so you rank in every zip code in your service territory, not just the one your office is in. Together they give you total territory control: you're targeting people at competitor locations (geofencing) and showing up when people search from home (geo-gridding).

Do I need a Digital HQ for the Content Crew?

Yes — HQ Lite minimum for the Content Crew. The Digital Promoter requires HQ Standard. The Block Captain and Neighborhood Captain require HQ Command. The HQ is the infrastructure all four positions run from.
