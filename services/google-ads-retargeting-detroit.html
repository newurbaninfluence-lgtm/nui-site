Google vs. Facebook: Why You Need Both

Facebook retargeting reaches people on social media. Google retargeting reaches them everywhere else — news sites, weather apps, recipe blogs, YouTube, Gmail. Together, they create an omnipresent effect: your prospect sees your brand on Instagram, then on CNN, then on YouTube, then in their Gmail sidebar. That level of visibility builds trust and drives conversions faster than either channel alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

GOOGLE ADS RETARGETING FAQ

What's the difference between Google and Facebook retargeting?

Facebook shows your ads on Facebook and Instagram. Google shows your ads across 2M+ websites, YouTube, Gmail, and apps. Facebook is better for visual, social engagement. Google is better for broad reach and catching people during work hours. Most businesses benefit from running both.

How much should I budget for Google Ads?

For retargeting only, $300-500/mo in ad spend is a solid starting point. If you want search ads (showing up when people Google your services), plan for $500-2,000/mo depending on your industry and competition. The $199/mo management fee is separate from ad spend.

Do I need a Google Ads account?

Yes, but we'll set it up for you if you don't have one. You own the account — we just manage it. This means if you ever stop working with us, you keep all your campaign data and audience lists.

How do you track conversions?

We install Google Tag Manager and set up tracking for phone calls, form submissions, purchases, and any other action you consider a conversion. You'll see exactly which ads and keywords are driving real results, not just clicks.
